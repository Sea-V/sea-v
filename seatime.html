What are the OOW <3000GT requirements? MCA MSN 1858 Amendment 2, Regulation II/1 — read the full breakdown.

Since the age of 16, a minimum of 36 months' onboard yacht service (any vessel size), including at least 365 days seagoing service on vessels 15 metres or over in load line length, made up of a minimum of 250 days' actual sea service and 115 days from any combination of actual sea, stand-by, or yard service — stand-by service can never exceed that voyage's actual sea days (max 14 consecutive days counted at once), and yard service counts up to a maximum of 90 days total.

Based on MCA MSN 1858 (Amendment 2, 18 May 2026), Regulation II/1 — Officer of the Watch (OOW), Yachts less than 3000GT, Unlimited Area. This is a guide calculated from your logged day totals, not day-by-day consecutive tracking, and covers sea service only — the CoC also requires EDH, ancillary courses, professional exams, and the oral exam. Always confirm your exact qualifying service with the MCA or PYA before applying. Vessel length/GT are read from each linked vessel's Length/GT fields.

What are the Master <3000GT requirements? MCA MSN 1858 Amendment 2, Regulation II/2 — read the full breakdown.

While serving as OOW Yachts <3000GT, a minimum of 24 months' onboard yacht service as a Deck Officer, including at least 240 days watchkeeping service. All of this service must be on vessels 15 metres or over in load line length, and must include either 12 months on vessels 24 metres or over, or 6 months on vessels of 500 GT or more.

Based on MCA MSN 1858 (Amendment 2, 18 May 2026), Regulation II/2 — Master, Yachts less than 3000GT, Unlimited Area. This is a guide calculated from your logged day totals and covers sea service only — the CoC also requires an MCA Master <500GT CoC or equivalent modules/exams, ancillary certificates, and the oral exam. PYA / MCA testimonial checklist Open when preparing sea service for verification or CoC applications.
  • Record both start and end dates for every engagement — even if still onboard.
  • Do not overlap dates between entries; new testimonials should follow the last period.
  • Break down actual sea, standby, and yard service using MCA definitions.
  • Watchkeeping days should reflect approved watchkeeping service only.
  • Attach signed sea service testimonials (SST) — Captain, or Owner/Manager for Masters.
  • Use the PYA or Nautilus portal links above for official verification, or export CSV for your training manager.
